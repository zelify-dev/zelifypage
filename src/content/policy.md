# Money Laundering

Laundering also called money laundering, laundering or money laundering; It constitutes one of the most serious illicit phenomena that can affect the economy and security of a country. It consists of hiding, converting, transferring, entering money of illegal origin within the legal circuit of trade in goods and services; and the ﬁnancing is in any type of currency or other type of asset.

Money laundering generally involves a set of multiple transactions to disguise the illicit origin or destination of funds, so that criminals can use said assets without committing themselves.

Money laundering usually involves a complex series of transactions, which are divided into three stages:

- - - - **Placement:** The introduction of illegally obtained funds or other valuables into ﬁnancial or non-ﬁnancial institutions.
            - **Diversiﬁcation:** Separate the income of criminal activity from its source, through ﬁnancial and non-ﬁnancial operations.
            - **Integration:** Replacing the laundered proceeds into the economy in such a way that it re-enters the ﬁnancial system or economy and appears as legitimate funds.

# Financing of Terrorism and Other Crimes

Terrorism can be deﬁned as: any act intended to cause death or bodily injury to a civilian or anyone not directly participating in hostilities, or to cause damage to public or private infrastructure, being purpose of said act, to intimidate a population or force a government or an international organization to carry out an act or to refrain from doing so.

Terrorist ﬁnancing consists of providing or collecting funds, in a ﬁctitious and deliberate manner, with the intention that they will be used or knowing that they will be used to commit a terrorist act, manifested in three stages:

- Income from legitimate or illegitimate investments.
- Funds used for a purpose, with beneﬁts to a crime.
- Commitment of acts of terror.

  - **1988 VIENNA CONVENTION:** This convention seeks to be a complete, effective and operational instrument, speciﬁcally directed against illicit trafﬁcking.
  - **PALERMO CONVENTION OF 2000:** Prevent and combat human trafﬁcking, with special attention to women and children.
  - **Political Declaration and Plan of Action adopted by the General Assembly in June 1998** , which underlines the importance of strengthening international, regional and subregional cooperation to combat money laundering.

### Global Anti-Money Laundering Program

The Unit to combat money laundering and ﬁnancing of terrorism focuses its efforts on providing technical assistance and training to all member states in judicial and ﬁnancial measures and legal control. Through the Group of Experts, regulations are developed a model on money laundering crimes related to drug trafficking and other crimes.

## OPERATIONS REVIEW PROCEDURE

In the case of operations involving natural persons, the client's general information will be reviewed and validated.

If unusualness is determined, the Compliance Ofﬁcer will request the respective additional documentation from the client to further his analysis.

When analyzing unusual operations, the following elements must be taken into consideration:

- The veracity and ease of veriﬁcation of the additional information provided by the client or company.
- The client's willingness to provide the additional information requested.
- The country where the client carries out its activities.
- Information that can be obtained through other companies.
- The information that can be obtained from public databases, such as the press, the Internet or other bases.

## UNUSUAL OPERATIONS REPORTING PROCEDURE

All operations carried out or intended to be carried out, which are not related to the client's economic activity or their proﬁle, will be included in the register of unusual operations, regardless of their amount.

When an employee detects an unusual operation, he or she will report it by email, under absolute conﬁdentiality, to the Compliance Ofﬁcer so that he or she can analyze it and take the corresponding actions, with the following data:

- Name or company name of the client.
- Type and number of identity document.
- Amount of units and purchase amount.
- Operation date.

The Compliance Ofﬁcer will prepare documentation with the following:

- Identiﬁcation of the client, indicating in the case of natural persons: full name, date of birth, type and number of the identity document, nationality, profession or occupation and address and telephone number. And in the case of legal entities: name or company name, taxpayer registration number, address, telephone number and legal representative.
- Indicate if the client has previously carried out a suspicious operation.
- List and description of the operations carried out, mentioning dates, amounts, place of execution and supporting documents that are attached as transfers of funds.
- Irregularities and considerations that led to classifying said operations as suspicious.

Suspects are treated:

- Customer operations that, due to their number, quantity or characteristics, may reasonably lead to suspicion that they are using the company for illicit purposes.

## INFORMATION RESERVATION

In the event that any international or national authority competent in the prevention of money laundering and/or ﬁnancing of terrorism requests from Zwipetech Inc. information about a client, intermediary, ofﬁcial, the Compliance Ofﬁcer of Zwippetech Inc. will support and collaborate. for the provision of relevant information.

Therefore, the Compliance Ofﬁcer will keep the respective ﬁles of the information provided by clients, intermediaries, in perfect chronological order.

It will maintain the ﬁles for a period of 10 years from the date of the last transaction or contractual relationship.

Neither Zwipetech Inc. nor its directors nor its staff will inform clients who have carried out or are attempting to carry out suspicious transactions, that they have sent information about them to the competent authority that formally requested it in writing. Rejected operations, due to their possible suspicious nature, will be kept by Zwipetech Inc. staff under complete conﬁdentiality.
